The Art of Discernment

Healing Through Poetry

Two hours with two poems — one written two centuries ago, one written in reply. A slow, live reading that quietly teaches the one discernment beneath all healing: knowing when to yield, when to stand, and when to walk away.

What this masterclass is

Most of us, somewhere in our lives, are quietly confused about a single question: when something presses on us, when someone wounds us, when a relationship asks too much of us — should we yield, should we hold our ground, or should we walk away?

We get it wrong in both directions. We submit when we should not, because some part of us cannot yet believe we deserve better. Or we strike back so hard that we are destroyed alongside what we were fighting. The middle path — standing firm without being consumed — is the hardest to find, and the least taught.

This masterclass approaches that question through poetry. Across two hours, we read two poems slowly together — and through them, something becomes clear that is difficult to name in the abstract but unmistakable once you have felt it. By God's grace, it is the discernment I believe lies beneath all genuine healing.

This is not a lecture about poetry, and it is not therapy. It is a slow, lived reading — the kind that meets you where you are, and leaves you a little more able to tell the difference between surrender, sovereignty, and self-destruction.

Healing does not come without the power of discernment.

What you will carry away

  1. A way of reading poetry slowly — the kind of attention that turns a poem into a mirror rather than a museum piece.
  2. The discernment to recognise, in your own relationships and pressures, which of the three responses you are standing in.
  3. A felt understanding of the difference between self-restraint and self-abandonment — two things that look alike from the outside and could not be more different within.
  4. A quiet experience of how poetry can hold what ordinary advice cannot — and how it can become part of how you heal.
  5. Two hours of stillness and depth, in a small live room, with no pressure to perform or share — only to receive.

Why poetry, why now

We tend to think of poetry as a luxury — something for people with time, or for students of literature. But the people who perform at the highest levels often know better.

The renowned heart surgeon Dr. Devi Shetty has spoken of how, in the height of a high-pressure operation, he experiences himself as an artist — an instrument in God's hands. The precision is technical; the steadiness is spiritual.

On surgery as a spiritual art

Anand Mahindra has said that whether one is an engineer or an MBA, everyone should study the arts — because the arts engage the whole brain, and a whole brain makes better decisions.

On the arts and the full mind

Poetry read slowly is not an escape from a demanding life. It is a way of bringing the whole self — mind, heart, and spirit — into how we live and decide and heal. This masterclass is for anyone who senses that, however demanding their work or their wounds, there is a quieter intelligence waiting to be met.

Who this is for

  • Anyone walking a path of healing — from a relationship, a loss, a pattern they are only beginning to name.
  • Those drawn to poetry as something deeper than craft — as a way of meeting themselves.
  • Professionals — doctors, engineers, builders, leaders — who carry quiet weight and sense that the arts have something to offer their inner life.
  • Readers of Indian and world poetry who want the slow, spiritually-rooted reading they rarely find elsewhere.
  • Anyone confused, in some part of their life, about whether to submit, to stand, or to strike back — and ready to learn the discernment between them.
